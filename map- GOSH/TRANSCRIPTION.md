## ✅ RESOLVED — Morgan Stanley level numbering has NO offset
Sources: `IMG_2638.jpg` (Premier Inn Fisk sheet 1/2, Levels 1–4), plus re-reading
`IMG_2560/2637` (Morgan Stanley Levels −1 to 3).

Three independent facts settle it:

1. **The Premier Inn Fisk sheets use public numbering.** `IMG_2638` (FF-FZ, Rev A, 05.07.24)
   is titled "PREMIER INN CLINICAL BUILDING · FIRE ZONE LAYOUT SHEET 1/2 · LEVEL 1–4", and its
   Level 5/6/7 companions (K5/K6/K7-FIR) match the lift directory's wards exactly.
2. **The two buildings must share numbering.** They are one complex (Mittal Children's Medical
   Centre) with shared lift lobbies; the directory board lists both under a single Level column.
3. **The Morgan Stanley Level 2 sheet shows the garden.** Its bottom-right carries a distinctly
   organic, curvilinear landscaped area — the **Morgan Stanley Garden**, which the site boards
   place at Level 2 (ground). A drawing labelled "LEVEL 2" containing the Level 2 garden cannot
   be offset.

**Conclusion: Morgan Stanley Fisk "Level N" = public "Level N" for N = 1…7.** The extra sheets
are simply storeys with no public destinations, so they never appear on the lift directory:
- **Level 0** and **Level −1** — two basement/service storeys below public Level 1 (the site
  slopes; Level 2 is the street-level ground floor, so two levels below Level 1 is plausible)
- **Level 8** — plant/roof above Level 7 (its sheet is sparse, consistent with plant)

**Do not apply any offset when assigning floors.** The current gosh.ts floor indices are correct
on this axis.
